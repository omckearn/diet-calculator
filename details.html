How the scores are assembled, in plain English.

What the scores mean

Each food category has two numbers: a Health score and an Environment score. The Health score uses a 0–2 scale, while the Environment score reflects impact on a 0–2 scale with one decimal. Your totals are the sum across all 16 categories.

Health score

Higher points reflect foods linked with better dietary outcomes. The stoplight colors map to low (red), medium (yellow), and high (green) scores.

Environment score

Higher points indicate a lower environmental footprint. Small differences are shown with a single decimal so the scale stays sensitive to food choices.

Why frequency matters

The frequency options are ordered from least to most, so your selection determines how many points that category contributes to each total.
